Description


The Airforce is the military branch which focuses on all aspects of air power. This includes 5 core missions including air superiority, global strike, rapid global mobility, intelligence; surveillance and reconnaissance; and command and control. Its mission is "to fly, fight and win - airpower anytime, anywhere".

History


The U.S. War Department created the first antecedent of the U.S. Air Force, as a part of the U.S. Army, in 1907, which through a succession of changes of organization, titles, and missions advanced toward eventual independence 40 years later.


The National Security Act of 1947 was signed on 26 July 1947 by President Harry S. Truman, which established the Department of the Air Force, but it was not until 18 September 1947, when the first secretary of the Air Force, W. Stuart Symington, was sworn into office that the Air Force was officially formed as an independent service branch


Some of the main wars that the Air Force contributed in are World War 1, World War 2, Cold War, Korean War, Vietnam War, Gulf War, and Iraq Wars.


Predecessor organizations to todays Air Force:

  • Aeronautical Division, Signal Corps (1 August 1907 - 18 July 1914)
  • Aviation Section, Signal Corps (18 July 1914 - 20 May 1918)
  • Division of Military Aeronautics (20 May 1918 to 24 May 1918)
  • U.S. Army Air Service (24 May 1918 to 2 July 1926)
  • U.S. Army Air Corps (2 July 1926 to 20 June 1941)
  • U.S. Army Air Forces (20 June 1941 to 18 September 1947)
